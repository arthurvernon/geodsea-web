Boat Owner

Geo d'Sea allows owners to register a vessel and to then manage those who may skipper it. The vessel details are provided to the SRO in the event of the vessel not logging off so that the SRO can then take the necessary steps to confirm the status of the vessel and if necessary to launch a search and rescue mission.

Background

When you register a vessel, you are describing the vessel, who owns it and who has the authority to use it. Without this information, the mobile application will not allow the skipper to commence a trip on this vessel.

A boat may be owned by:

You alone
As you register, choose the "owned by me" option.
You and other people known to you
Register yourself (choose the "owned by me" option). Later on you can add others.
An organisation
Choose the "owned by an organisation" option and select the appropriate organisation from the list. Note: You must be an active manager within the organisation to be able to register a vessel.

A vessel may be skippered by people the owner allows. When the owner is:

  • An organisation, then any manager within that organisation may allow any person within the organisation to skipper the vessel.
  • You, then you may allow any other person known to you to skipper the vessel.

If there are multiple owners, then only one owner needs to grant a skipper access.

A skipper may be granted a right to use the vessel indefinitely or for a limited period of time. Additionally, the right to use the vessel may be temporarily suspended by marking the skipper as suspended.

Registration

When you first register the vessel, you need to enter the details of the vessel and at least one owner. Other owners and skippers can be added later.

A boat can be registered online using by clicking on this link or going via the Add Vessel menu (above).

The Vessel Registration form provides the option to enter all details manually, or auto-populate vessel details from the licensing authority.

When all the required information has been entered, press the "register" button and you are done.

Auto-Populate

A licensing authority can provide a service that will allow the vessel details to be downloaded from their site.

To do this, click on the "get from license" button at the top of the form

The system will make a best guess who the relevant licensing authority is, based upon your home address. If this is wrong, then just select the "Wrong agency?" checkbox and choose the right agency from the list.

If the suggested licensing authority or one you select does not provide this service then you will receive a message:
No support
If this is the case then just press the "cancel" button.

Otherwise, enter your license or registration number as it appears on your license and press the "lookup" button. If the request can be completed successfully, then all that is left to do is complete the storage location and press theregister button.

Updating Vessel Information

If the auto-populate option is not chosen then all fields with the exception of the skippers will need to be completed. If the auto-populate is chosen then you need to still define the storage location and at least one owner.

The only exception to the above is that a non-motorised vessel does not require a fuel capacity.

Any skipper defined whilst registering the vessel will automatically have a right to use the vessel. If this is inappropriate, then the skipper can later be suspended or a time period for imposed.

Updating Information

Details of the vessel, its owners and the rights of skippers can be maintained once the vessel has been registered.

Limitations

The following restrictions apply to updates:

HIN
This must be unique and a change will be rejected if a vessel with this number is already registered
Owner
At least one owner must be defined. An owner may be a person, a number of people or an organisation.
Skipper
A time period may either or both a lower and an upper time limit. If both are specified then the upper limit must be after the lower limit. If a lower limit is defined then the skipper can only commence a trip on the vessel after that time. If an upper limit is specified then the skipper may only commence a trip prior to that time.
