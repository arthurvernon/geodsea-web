Introduction

Search and rescue operations (SRO) can utilise Geo d'Sea to track boating activity within their area of interest.

Journeys may be registered, updated and closed out by the SRO using the the Rescue portal. When boats are overdue, the portal will alert the SRO.

Alternatively, skippers may keep the SRO informed by using one of the available mobile applications. These can be downloaded from the iTunes or Google App store.

Setup of an SRO entails:

  1. User registration
  2. Organisation registration
  3. Application by the organisation to perform the search and rescue role
  4. Approval by the relevant authority for the organisation to perform the search and rescue role
  5. Addition of members to the organisation

User Registration

You are already registered. Others within the SRO may need to do the same.

Before you can register a SRO, you must first be a registered user of Geo d'Sea.

If you are not already registered, then go to the user registration page. Upon completing the form, an email will be sent to the email address you specified. In it will be a link that you need to click on to complete your registration process.

User registration is free.

To complete the rest of the registration process, log onto the system and complete the remainder of the steps.

Organisation Registration

A SRO must first define the organisation before it can establish the search and rescue role. To do this, visit the organisation registration page.

The user who registered the organisation is automatically added as a member of that organisation.

Adding Search and Rescue Role

Geo d'Sea recognizes a number of different roles for organisations. One of those roles is search and rescue. Another role is that of the authority that oversees SROs.

Prior to an SRO role being recognised, the relevant authority must approve the information supplied by the SRO.

The important aspects of setting up this role are as follows.

Area of Operations:

This is the region over which the SAR assumes responsibility for boating.

When a skipper uses the mobile app to start this journey, the system will then report this plan to the SROs who have indicated a primary responsibility for that region.

Contact Information:

Geo d'Sea maintains important contact information on behalf of SROs and provides this information to users via the mobile application. This includes the radio call sign and a telephone number that the SRO can be reached on.

For the purposes of the relevant Sea Rescue Authority, the organisation's primary point of contact should also be defined.

Location Update Preference:

By default, boats will only inform the SRO of their departure and completion of their journey.

The SRO may request that boats, departing from their Area of Operation, provide location reports. Skippers may agree to report their location as requested, they may vary the request in order to conserve resources or they may refuse to report their location.

The application will then attempt comply with the skipper's conditions and provide reports to the SRO. This is of course subject to the skipper having the application running and the phone's resource constraints, e.g. battery, memory, GPS and network signal.

SRO Local search and rescue organisations maintain records of boating activities originating from within their area of interest. They then maintain a watch on their area or interest using maps and status displaystracked.

SARs can use Geo Tracker Sea Rescue Map to view active boats in their area of interest.

Traditionally this information was maintained in a log, updated when a skipper radioed in his intentions and later on again when the skipper returned. This information can now be maintained within Geo d'Sea.

Skippers who use the BoatTrack® application will input this information directly, reducing the burden on members to record details.

Rescue organisations are alerted to cases where a boat fails to report in by the planned return time.

When a club first registers to use GeoDsea, the details of the club are entered including the radio callsign and other contact details for the club. Rescue organisation can define and publish the location of natural and man-made items such as hazards, channel markers and boat launching ramps. Once published, this information can be used by skippers. For example, a skipper using GeoDSea can see and enter his current location as "Hillarys Marina Boat Ramp" as opposed to a geographic coordinate.
Also within an area of interest, the organisation has the ability to vet and publish (make public) or discard information that others wish to make public.

Using Information

When a boat reports a position within a certain distance of an object then the system can raise an alert to the skipper. For example:

  • An exclusion zone may be defined around a sanctuary.
  • A 50 metre radius around a submerged object.

Monitoring

By enabling real-time reporting, others can track your location. This is ideal for:

  • Event organisers wanting a low-cost solution for tracking vessels, e.g. a sailing regatta to plot yacht locations.
  • Friends and family who want some assurance of your location without being seen to be prying.
