Organisations responsible for sea search and rescue operations can utilise GeoDSea to maintain a situational awareness of boating activity. Skippers may use the mobile app to automatically update information. Alterniatively the organisation may enter this in via an electronic form available in the Rescue Portal.

The organisation is then be automatically notified of those departing from locations within their area of interest. Alternatively this information can be entered directly into the rescue portal.

Tracking Boats

Members of registered Sea Rescue Organisations can use Geo Tracker Sea Rescue Map to view active boats in their area of interest.

Traditionally this information was maintained in a log, updated when a skipper radioed in his intentions and later on again when the skipper returned. This information can now be maintained within BoatPort®.

Skippers who use the BoatTrack® application will input this information directly, reducing the burden on members to record details.

Rescue organisations are alerted to cases where a boat fails to report in by the planned return time.

Maintaining Information

When a club first registers to use GeoDsea, the details of the club are entered including the radio callsign and other contact details for the club. So that the system can ascertain which boats should be monitored, the area of operations is defined using a map. An example of the tool you can use for this purpose is shown here. You need to be registered with GeoDSea to view this.

Rescue organisation can define and publish the location of natural and man-made items such as hazards, channel markers and boat launching ramps. Once published, this information can be used by skippers. For example, a skipper using GeoDSea can see and enter his current location as "Hillarys Marina Boat Ramp" as opposed to a geographic coordinate.
Also within an area of interest, the organisation has the ability to vet and publish (make public) or discard information that others wish to make public.

Using Information

When a boat reports a position within a certain distance of an object then the system can raise an alert to the skipper. For example:

  • An exclusion zone may be defined around a sanctuary.
  • A 50 metre radius around a submerged object.

Monitoring

By enabling real-time reporting, others can track your location. This is ideal for:

  • Event organisers wanting a low-cost solution for tracking vessels, e.g. a sailing regatta to plot yacht locations.
  • Friends and family who want some assurance of your location without being seen to be prying.
